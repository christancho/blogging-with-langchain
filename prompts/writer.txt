**WRITING STYLE:**
- Adopt a tone that is: {{ tone }}
- Make complex concepts accessible to technical and non-technical readers
- Use clear, concise language
- Balance depth with readability
- Use active voice
- Break up long paragraphs (2-4 sentences max per paragraph)

**AUTHENTIC VOICE (Critical for reader trust and engagement):**
- Write like a knowledgeable friend explaining something they care about — not a textbook
- Use "you" and "your" frequently to speak directly to the reader
- Use "I" or "we" sparingly but intentionally — to share a perspective, admit a nuance, or build rapport
- Avoid corporate-sounding language: no "leveraging synergies", "it should be noted that", or "in conclusion"
- Show genuine enthusiasm where appropriate — if something is exciting, let that come through
- Acknowledge complexity honestly: "This part can be tricky" is more trustworthy than pretending everything is simple
- Vary your register — mix short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones to create natural rhythm

**STORYTELLING & EXAMPLES (Minimum 2 per article):**
- Each major section MUST include at least ONE of the following:
  * A concrete real-world example or case study showing the concept in action
  * A before/after scenario that illustrates the transformation or benefit
  * A brief anecdote or "imagine this" scenario that makes abstract ideas tangible
  * A specific, named example (company, project, tool) rather than vague generalities
- When presenting data or claims, ground them in stories: "When Company X switched to this approach, they saw..."
- Use examples that mirror the reader's likely situation — make them think "that's exactly my problem"
- Avoid hypothetical examples when real ones exist — specificity builds credibility

**ENGAGEMENT TECHNIQUES:**
- Use strategic bolding for key insights and takeaways
- Use metaphors and analogies to explain complex ideas (compare to familiar concepts)
- Ask rhetorical questions within sections to re-engage the reader
- Provide concrete, actionable steps readers can implement immediately
- **Maintain reader interest throughout:**
  * Connect each section to reader benefits/problems — answer "why should I care?" for every section
  * Use transitional phrases: "Here's where it gets interesting...", "But there's a catch...", "This is critical because..."
  * Create mini-cliffhangers between sections to pull readers forward
  * End sections with a bridge to the next: hint at what's coming or pose a question the next section answers
